Maps

Everyone loves a good map! My geospatial algorithm matches voting precincts with college campuses using shapefiles. By leveraging student location data (latitude and longitude), we identify which precincts intersect, overlap, or surround each campus, facilitating campus specific targetting and statistical aggregation.

Every campus is connected through official IPEDS identifiers, ensuring data accuracy across our entire national database. While our interactive map showcases select campuses, our proprietary algorithm processes data for thousands of schools nationwide, handling complex scenarios like multi-campus precincts and low-voter areas with sophisticated edge-case logic.

Data Pipelines

Everyone loves a good piping! Copper CRM and EveryAction don't natively integrate, so I built a custom data pipeline to connect them. I created multiple python scripts that automatically mirror data between the systems—so regardless of where a record originates, it populates in both places and stays synchronized in real-time.

The pipeline pulls data from multiple sources: Copper, EveryAction, and Google Sheets all flow into BigQuery, which serves as the central hub. From there, the data syncs back out to both systems, keeping everything in sync. This eliminates manual data entry, reduces errors, and ensures the team always has access to complete, up-to-date supporter records across all platforms.

Voter Targeting

After knocking on kajillions of doors as a canvasser, I became increasingly interested with each knock in the data that determined which voters I was talking to. Now, I get to make the lists! Muahahaha!

And, now I know that it's a balance of resources and objectives. Many organizations think voter registration via text is a waste of resources, but with smart targeting and capture tools like Rock the Vote (RTV), we can contact the voters most likely to need updating or re-registering and track our results to calculate cost per registration.

The cost per registration for this universe was around $28, and that's just the submissions we can link back to records. We do gather more, but not all submissions linked to this campaign (via UTM codes) can be linked to specific text records. If the name they used to register is different than the one on file, or if they didn't use a phone number on their registration, we can't link it. Some people also get the text and register on the state's site or a privacy setting blocks the UTMs. Those records can be loosely correlated with a voter file field like voter_registration_date, but not all states and counties update that field consistently. 

-- This query will pull together a list of records from both the voterfile and our everyaction records for voter registration
-- There are 5 key target buckets
    -- unregistered records on the voterfile placed with in precincts overlapping specific campuses 
    -- voters who have likely moved and not yet updated their registration 
    -- voters who haven't udpated their voter registration since 2023 and did not vote in either 2024 and 2025 
    -- voters who registered when they turned 18, and haven't udpated since ((likely to have registered at parents house))
    -- people in our everyaction database that return no match on the voterfile

-- transformations and appends to the voterfile happen before this step 
-- ideology score is c3 permissible screen, while 40 appears low it does not function with the same polarity as partisanship scores 


Create or replace table proj-tmc-mem-ng.Distributed_2026_Main.2026_summer_vr_universe_v1 as (

with campus_peeps as (  --## campus VAP who are not registered somewhere else

  select distinct
      
    c.*,
    case when c.campus_name is not null then 'campus_vap' end as `target_bucket`

from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.universes.2026_distributed`  c

where c.vb_tsmart_state in ('AZ','MI','NC','NM','NV','PA','GA','IA')
and c.presence_2026 = 'Yes'  -- limits to only campuses within NGA focus -- campuses are joined to the voterfile using tmc_national_precinct_id
and c.vb_voterbase_registration_status = 'Unregistered'
and (c.vb_voterbase_mover_registration_status = 'Not Registered' or c.vb_voterbase_mover_registration_status is null)
),


movers as (  --##movers who did not register in another state

  select distinct
      
    c.*,
    'movers' as `target_bucket`

from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.universes.2026_distributed`  c
left join campus_peeps cp on c.cell = cp.cell

where c.vb_tsmart_state in ('AZ','MI','NC','NM','NV','PA','GA','IA')
and c.vb_voterbase_mover_status != 'Resides at Current Address' 
and c.vb_voterbase_mover_status != 'Moved out of State'
and c.ts_tsmart_ideology_score > 40
and (c.vb_voterbase_mover_registration_status = 'Not Registered' or c.vb_voterbase_mover_registration_status is null)
and c.age between 18 and 29
and cp.cell is null

),


lapsed as ( --##people who haven't updated their and did not vote in 2024 or 2025 + ideology screen
  
  select distinct
      
    c.*,
   'lapsers' as `target_bucket`

from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.universes.2026_distributed`  c
left join `proj-tmc-mem-ng.targetsmart_enhanced.enh_targetsmart__ntl_current` vf on c.vb_voterbase_id = vf.vb_voterbase_id
left join campus_peeps cp on c.cell = cp.cell
left join movers mm on c.cell = mm.cell

where c.vb_tsmart_state in ('AZ','MI','NC','NM','NV','PA','GA','IA')
and c.vb_vf_registration_date < '2023-01-01'
and c.age between 18 and 29
and vf.vb_vf_g2024 is null
and vf.vb_vf_g2025 is null
and vf.ts_tsmart_ideology_score > 40
and cp.cell is null
and mm.cell is null
),


first_time_reg_needing_updates as ( -- ## registration records where the first time seen = the registration date (they havent registered to vote again since their first time ever) and aged 18 - 22 
                                                  -- thinking these are people who move every year 
                                                  -- registered for presidential 

  select distinct
      
    c.*,
   'first_time_seen' as `target_bucket`

from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.universes.2026_distributed`  c
left join `proj-tmc-mem-ng.targetsmart_enhanced.enh_targetsmart__ntl_current` vf on c.vb_voterbase_id = vf.vb_voterbase_id
left join campus_peeps cp on c.cell = cp.cell
left join movers mm on c.cell = mm.cell
left join lapsed l on c.cell = l.cell

where c.vb_tsmart_state in ('AZ','MI','NC','NM','NV','PA','GA','IA')
and PARSE_DATE('%Y%m%d', CAST(vf.vb_firsttimeseen_voterid AS STRING)) = c.vb_vf_registration_date
and c.vb_vf_registration_date < '2025-01-01'
and c.age between 18 and 22
and vf.ts_tsmart_ideology_score > 40
and cp.cell is null
and mm.cell is null
and l.cell is null

),


ea_non_matches as (

  select 
    c.*,
    'ea_no_voter_match' as `target_bucket` 
    
  from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.universes.2026_distributed` c
  left join campus_peeps cp on c.cell = cp.cell
  left join movers mm on c.cell = mm.cell
  left join lapsed l on c.cell = l.cell
  left join first_time_reg_needing_updates f on c.cell = f.cell

  where c.cell is not null
  and c.vb_voterbase_id is null
  and c.ea_state in ('AZ','MI','NC','NM','NV','PA','GA','IA')
  and cast(c.ea_vanid as int64) in (select vanid from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.match.ea_base_matched` where vaddress is not null)
  and cast(c.ea_vanid as int64) in (select vanid from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.raw_bonterra__ng_cloud.tsm_nextgen_contactscontacts_mym` where DateCreated > '2022-01-01')
  and cp.cell is null
  and mm.cell is null
  and l.cell is null
  and f.cell is null
),


combined as (
  select * from campus_peeps
  union all 
  select * from movers 
  union all
  select * from lapsed
  union all 
  select * from first_time_reg_needing_updates
  union all
  select * from ea_non_matches
),


refined as (

  select distinct 

    target_bucket,
    cell,
    first_name,
    last_name,
    vb_voterbase_id,
    vb_smartvan_id, 
    ea_vanid, 
    turfed_state,
    age

from combined)


select 
  turfed_state,
  target_bucket, 
  count(distinct cell) as phones
  
  from refined 

where turfed_state in  ('AZ','MI','NC','NM','NV','PA','GA','IA')


group by all


order by 1,2,3 asc;)

---------------------------------------  creating a sub universe for a frequency experiment in GA, we were looking at registration counts across multiple passes  ((Group A got 1 touch, Group B got 3))

create or replace table `proj-tmc-mem-ng.Distributed_2026_Main.summer_vr_universe_5202026_GA_Frequency_Experiment_Groups` as (

WITH base AS (
  select
    vr.target_bucket,
    vr.cell,
    vr.first_name,
    COALESCE(
      INITCAP(
        NULLIF(
          TRIM(REGEXP_REPLACE(vr.first_name, r"[^a-zA-Z' ]", '')),
        '')
      ),
      'friend'
    ) AS formatted_name,
    vr.last_name,
    vr.vb_voterbase_id,
    vr.vb_smartvan_id,
    vr.ea_vanid,
    vr.turfed_state AS state,
    vr.age,
    vf.vb_voterbase_gender,
    vf.vb_vf_race,
    vf.vb_vf_cd
  FROM `proj-tmc-mem-ng.Distributed_2026_Main.summer_vr_universe_5202026` vr
  LEFT JOIN `proj-tmc-mem-ng.targetsmart_enhanced.enh_targetsmart__ntl_current` vf
    ON vr.vb_voterbase_id = vf.vb_voterbase_id
  WHERE vr.turfed_state = 'GA'
),

age_helper AS (
  select
    *,
    -- Bucket age so strata aren't too granular
    CASE
      WHEN age < 25 THEN '18-24'
      WHEN age < 35 THEN '25-34'
      WHEN age < 45 THEN '35-44'
      WHEN age < 55 THEN '45-54'
      WHEN age < 65 THEN '55-64'
      WHEN age >= 65 THEN '65+'    -- we did not screen age on our ea records, so there were a few folx from these age brackes 
      ELSE 'unknown'
    END AS age_bucket
  FROM base
),

assigned AS (     -- this step balances the two comparison groups on age, gender, race and geography 
  select
    *,
    ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
      PARTITION BY
        age_bucket,
        COALESCE(vb_voterbase_gender, 'unknown'),
        COALESCE(vb_vf_race, 'unknown'),
        COALESCE(vb_vf_cd, 'unknown')
      ORDER BY RAND()
    ) AS strata_rn
  FROM age_helper
)

select
  * except(strata_rn),
  case when mod(strata_rn, 2) = 0 then 'Group A' else 'Group B' end AS assigned_group   -- assigns a group to each record at random
from assigned
order by formatted_name)


--------------------------------------- ga round 3 -- We removed a hanful of people who completed a voter registration action in Rock the Vote or were tagged as wrong number, hostile, etc by the texters each pass


with rtv AS (   -- gathers records from rock the vote
  SELECT 
    REGEXP_REPLACE(phone, r'\D', '') AS phone, 
    lower(first_name) AS first_name,
    lower(last_name)  AS last_name,
    status

  FROM `proj-tmc-mem-ng.RTV_2026_Main.rtv_reporting_2026_vanid`
  where (utm_campaign = '25_Distributed_GOTV_c3' or utm_source = 'distributed')
),

removals as (  -- gathers tag data from scale to win 
  select 
  phone_number
    
  from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.metrics.stw_text_tags_by_campaign`
  where 1=1 
  and (campaign_title = '6/29/2026_GA_Voter Registration_MyVoters_c3_distributed' or campaign_title = '7/06/2026_GA_Voter Registration Exp_MyVoters_c3_distributed')
  and lower(name) in ('wrong number','hostile','contact fatigue','contact info update','updated registration','moved')

)

select 
  target_bucket,
  'summer_vr' as universe,
  cell,
  formatted_name as first_name,
  last_name,
  vb_voterbase_id,
  vb_smartvan_id,
  ea_vanid,
  state,
  assigned_group

from `proj-tmc-mem-ng.Distributed_2026_Main.summer_vr_universe_5202026_GA_Frequency_Experiment_Groups`

where assigned_group = 'Group B'

and cell not in (select phone from rtv where phone is not null)   -- removes records from the text list that match back to rock the vote submissions on phone
and lower(first_name || last_name) not in (select first_name || last_name from rtv) -- removes records from the text list that match back to rock the vote submissions on first name + last name exact match
and cell not in (select phone_number from removals)  -- removes the tagged folx
