Maps

My innovative geospatial algorithm intelligently matches voting precincts with college campuses using advanced shapefile technology. By leveraging student location data (latitude and longitude), we precisely identify which precincts intersect, overlap, or surround each campus, producing unprecedented insight into the college voter landscape.

Every campus is seamlessly connected through official IPEDS identifiers, ensuring data accuracy across our entire national database. While our interactive maps showcase select campuses, our proprietary algorithm processes data for thousands of schools nationwide, handling complex scenarios like multi-campus precincts and low-voter areas with sophisticated edge-case logic.

Data Pipelines

Copper CRM and EveryAction don't natively integrate, so I built a custom data pipeline to connect them. I created multiple scripts that automatically sync data bidirectionally between the systems—so regardless of where a record originates, it populates in both places and stays synchronized in real-time.

The pipeline pulls data from multiple sources: Copper, EveryAction, and Google Sheets all flow into BigQuery, which serves as the central hub. From there, the data syncs back out to both systems, keeping everything in sync. This eliminates manual data entry, reduces errors, and ensures the team always has access to complete, up-to-date supporter records across all platforms.

About Scott

Scott has been organizing for over 20 years. He started in politics at 18 as a paid petitioner, then spent years canvassing and organizing around Cincinnati, Ohio.

From there, he worked as a Canvass Director at Fieldworks and eventually became the National IE Field Director at Community Outreach Group (now Scale to Change), which ran the field for Planned Parenthood as its in-house vendor. In 2020, he switched from field to digital, running the Sierra Club's Digital Distributed organizing program during the pandemic, helping thousands of volunteers write letters, make calls, and send texts from home.

In 2021, he took a data job at For Our Future to learn more about the work and systems behind the scenes. After the 2022 midterms, he went back to school to fill in knowledge gaps, learning Python, SQL, and web development through a data science program at LA City College.

Today, he's Director of Engineering and Analytics at NextGen America, working on targeting, program analytics, and data pipelines.
